Submarine Monument

The Submarine Monument is the original version of the Indonesian Navy's Eastern Fleet Submarine Unit (Satselarmatim) KRI Pasopati 410 submarine. The biggest undersea monument in Asia is Monkasel. The Whiskey Class SS submarine KRI Pasopati 410 was constructed in 1952 at Vladivostok, Russia. On January 29, 1962, it joined the Indonesian Navy, whose primary mission was to cut off hostile sea routes of communication. Admiral Arief Kushariadi, the Chief of Staff of the Indonesian Navy, officially opened it on June 27, 1998. The public was able to access it on July 15, 1998.

Old Town Surabaya

Old Town Surabaya is a historical heritage site with the architectural appeal of magnificent colonial buildings, streets steeped in history, and a strong nostalgic atmosphere. Centered on Rajawali Street, the cluster of cultural heritage buildings in the area records the long history of Surabaya's development as a city since the 17th century. The Old Town of Surabaya is divided into four zones: the European Zone, the Chinatown Zone, the Arab Zone, and the Malay Zone. Let's explore the Old Town of Surabaya Beyond Heritage!

Kalimas Boat Tour

Kalimas Boat Tour is one of Surabaya's leading tourist destinations. The sunset atmosphere and the sparkling lights of the magnificent buildings look very beautiful when enjoyed from aboard this tour boat. Kalimas Boat Tour operates every day from 3:00 p.m. to 9:00 p.m. There are two departure piers, namely Taman Prestasi Pier and Monumen Kapal Selam Pier.
